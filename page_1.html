Manhole Covers

Many manhole covers around Wellington have company branding which can hint at what sort of cables lie below.

Small Circular Chorus Manhole Cover

Chorus build and maintain the majority of New Zealand's internet network. This manhole cover was quite small and the cover itself was made of plastic.

Small Circular Channell Manhole Cover

This manhole cover was the same size and materials as the previous Chorus one. The branding at the top says Channell and has the website channellcomm.com below, which returns a "Forbidden" message. It was hard to find information about this manhole cover but I did find a buried cable enclosure pit which sports this cover for sale at Transnet.

Large Telecom Manhole Cover

Now known as Spark, Telecom was formerly a State-Owned-Enterprise who were privatised in 1990. The company has a long history of providing telecommunications in New Zealand and own the ISP Xtra. Southern Cross Cable Ltd were half-owned by Telecom (still half owned by Spark) in 1998 when plans were made to build the Southern Cross Cable, the fibre-optic cable which connects New Zealand to the World Wide Web.

Medium Citylink Manhole Cover

Citylink are a company which began in the 1990s and installed fibre-optic cables for internet in the CBD in 1996. The cables originally ran along the trolley bus overhead system before it was decommissioned and dismantled.
